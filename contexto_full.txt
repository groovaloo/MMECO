# Moral Money Community System – Operational Model

## 1. Core Ethical Principle

The system is built around a single guiding rule:

Power and reputation come from real contributions to the survival and prosperity of the community.

Contribution can take different forms:
- Work
- Capital
- Knowledge
- Infrastructure
- Services

No form of contribution is inherently superior. What matters is the real impact on the system.

---

## 2. System Architecture

The ecosystem has three main layers.

### Layer 1 – Blockchain (Protocol Layer)

The blockchain is the source of truth.

It records:
- identities
- projects
- participation
- outcomes
- reputation
- governance decisions

All critical decisions and state changes happen inside the protocol.

AI agents cannot override the protocol.

---

### Layer 2 – AI Domain Agents

Specialized AI agents analyze contributions within specific domains.

Examples:
- agriculture
- construction
- housing
- manufacturing
- energy
- health
- logistics

Agents analyze project data and provide structured evaluations.

They help interpret information but do not hold authority.

---

### Layer 3 – Community Interaction

Members interact with the system mainly through a mobile interface.

Users can:
- create projects
- join projects
- confirm participation
- close projects

The system must remain simple for participants.

Complexity exists in the algorithm, not in user actions.

---

## 3. Projects as the Basic Unit

All activity inside the community is structured as projects.

Projects can be large or small.

Examples:
- building houses
- farming fields
- installing infrastructure
- repairing equipment
- helping paint a room

Each project contains:
- creator
- participants
- domain
- expected outcome

---

## 4. Project Lifecycle

1. Project creation  
2. Participants join  
3. Work execution  
4. Project closure  
5. System evaluation  

Participants confirm completion through their devices.

---

## 5. Evaluation Model

Constant rating of people is avoided to preserve freedom and social trust.

Evaluation only happens when necessary.

Three mechanisms exist.

### Normal completion

If all participants confirm completion, the project closes automatically.

Contribution is recorded without detailed evaluation.

### Structured feedback

If feedback is required, a short evaluation form can be used.

Possible dimensions include:

- effort
- quality
- reliability
- collaboration
- impact

Each dimension can be rated on a five-star scale.

### Conflict resolution

If participants disagree strongly, the case is escalated.

The system selects five people with the highest reputation in that domain to review the case.

---

## 6. Reputation System

Reputation is not global.

It is domain specific.

Examples:
- construction reputation
- agriculture reputation
- governance reputation

Reputation grows through:

- repeated successful participation
- high-impact projects
- technical competence

Reputation decreases when:

- contributions repeatedly cause problems
- projects require significant rework

---

## 7. Governance Model

There is no permanent political class.

Decision groups are dynamic.

When a decision is needed in a domain:

The system automatically selects the five individuals with the highest relevant reputation.

Those five form a temporary decision council.

After the decision, the council dissolves.

---

## 8. Anti-Manipulation Logic

The algorithm must detect and reduce manipulation risks.

Key strategies:

- weight evaluations based on evaluator reputation
- detect rating patterns between specific users
- reduce influence of suspicious clusters
- require stronger evidence for large impacts

---

## 9. Algorithmic Complexity Principle

Users experience simplicity.

They only:

- create
- join
- complete

All fairness mechanisms exist inside the algorithm.

The system must absorb complexity so that community life remains natural.

---

## 10. Design Goal

Create a decentralized community where:

- authority emerges from contribution
- governance is merit-based
- manipulation is difficult
- freedom between people is preserved

---

## 11. Community Bootstrapping (Small Community Phase)

The system must work even when the community is very small.

During early stages the algorithm cannot rely on large data histories.

The first members create projects and record participation exactly as in the mature system.

However reputation grows slowly and requires repeated participation across different projects.

If the system cannot find five highly reputable members in a domain, it selects the most relevant available members.

Example:

- if only three qualified members exist, those three form the council  
- if only two exist, the system expands the pool to related domains  

The goal is to allow decisions without blocking the system.

---

## 12. Long-Term Objective

The system aims to support decentralized communities where:

- authority emerges from real contribution
- governance adapts dynamically to competence
- economic exchange remains transparent
- manipulation becomes increasingly difficult

The algorithm carries the complexity so that daily life inside the community can remain simple, free and cooperative.

# 🧠 Memória Mestra do Ecossistema Moral Money

## 1. A Visão do Fundador (Nuno Cunha)
* **Não à Utopia Inútil:** Recusa de uma comunidade de "hippies" sem produtividade.
* **Não à Plutocracia:** Recusa de uma comunidade de "ricos" onde o dinheiro compra poder de decisão.
* **Justiça de Impacto:** O valor de um membro é medido pelo benefício real para a sobrevivência coletiva.
* **Equivalência Ética:** Um investidor (ex: 60 anos, 4M€ em infraestrutura/irrigação) tem o mesmo valor e soberania que um trabalhador ativo, pois ambos viabilizam a prosperidade do grupo.

## 2. Pilares do Projeto
* **Habitação:** LSF (Light Steel Frame), modular e expansível conforme o mérito/necessidade, sem estética "comunista" (casas iguais).
* **Economia:** Buildcoin via Blockchain (Substrate) para registo de ativos e reputação.
* **Governação:** Decisões técnicas por grupos de 5 especialistas validados pelo sistema.
* **Tecnologia:** IA Local (Ollama/Llama3) atuando como auditor ético e gestor de recursos.

## 3. Estado Atual do Sistema (2026-03-07)
* **Hardware:** MacBook Pro 2018 (i7, 16GB RAM) validado como capaz.
* **Ambiente Blockchain:** Rust e Wasm instalados.
* **Ambiente IA:** Python venv configurado; Ollama funcional com Llama3 (8B) carregado.
* **Primeiro Protótipo:** Criado o `agente_etica.py` para validar a equivalência Investidor/Trabalhador.

## 4. Notas Importantes para a Próxima Sessão
* O sistema deve ser exequível e documentado para atrair investidores/crowdfunding.
* A IA deve ser o "árbitro cego" que garante que o dinheiro não compra influência política.

## Estrutura Modular e Custo (2026-03-07)
* **Industrialização:** A estrutura modular é a única forma de atingir o menor custo possível através da produção em escala.
* **Flexibilidade de Escala:** Permite fazer uma casa pequena ou uma casa grande usando os mesmos componentes base.
* **Evolução:** Quem precisa de mais espaço ou contribui mais para a comunidade pode simplesmente adicionar novos módulos à sua habitação existente.

Moral Money – Reputation Algorithm

Overview

This document describes the computational model that implements the reputation system defined in reputation-system.md.

The algorithm converts participation in community projects into measurable reputation scores across multiple domains.


Core Variables

Each participant has a reputation vector.

Example:

R(user) = {
    construction: value,
    agriculture: value,
    energy: value,
    governance: value,
    health: value,
    logistics: value
}

Each value represents competence and trust level within that domain.


Project Contribution Model

Each project generates a contribution record.

Contribution = {
    user_id
    project_id
    domain
    role
    effort
    outcome
    feedback
}

These records form the basis for reputation calculation.


Reputation Update

After project completion, the system calculates a reputation change.

Example formula:

ΔR = BaseContribution × ImpactFactor × FeedbackScore

Where:

BaseContribution reflects participation level.
ImpactFactor reflects the importance of the project.
FeedbackScore reflects peer evaluation.


Reputation Decay

Reputation gradually decreases during long inactivity periods.

Example:

R(t) = R(t-1) × decay_factor

Decay should be slow and predictable.


Governance Selection

When governance is required in a domain:

1. The system ranks participants by reputation in that domain.
2. The top five are selected.
3. They form a temporary decision council.
4. After the decision, the council dissolves.


Anti-Manipulation Rules

The algorithm must detect and mitigate manipulation patterns.

Examples include:

- repeated mutual rating patterns
- sudden abnormal reputation spikes
- tightly connected evaluation clusters

Suspicious cases can be flagged for additional review.


Design Goal

The reputation algorithm must:

- reward real contribution
- recognize domain-specific competence
- prevent manipulation
- maintain fairness and transparency

Moral Money – Reputation System

Overview
The reputation system measures the real contribution of each participant to the community.
Reputation is domain-specific and grows through successful participation in projects.

Domain-Based Reputation

Each participant has separate reputation scores for different domains.

Examples of domains:

construction
agriculture
energy
governance
health
logistics

Reputation gained in one domain does not automatically transfer to another domain.

This ensures that expertise is recognized where it actually exists.

Reputation Growth

Reputation grows through successful participation in projects.

Key factors that increase reputation include:

- successful completion of tasks
- reliability and consistency
- positive feedback from collaborators
- contribution to high-impact projects

Reputation growth should not be linear.

High-impact projects may generate significantly larger reputation gains than routine activities.


Reputation Decay

Reputation should slowly decrease when a participant becomes inactive.

The purpose of reputation decay is to ensure that governance and influence remain connected to active participation in the community.

Decay should be gradual and predictable.

Possible decay rules may include:

- small periodic reductions in inactive domains
- slower decay for historically high contributors
- no decay for very recent activity

This mechanism prevents permanent accumulation of influence without continued contribution.


Reputation and Governance

Reputation directly influences governance participation.

When a decision is required in a specific domain, the system automatically selects the participants with the highest reputation in that domain.

The top five individuals form a temporary decision council.

This council reviews the case and makes a decision.

After the decision is made, the council dissolves automatically.

This mechanism ensures that authority emerges from demonstrated competence rather than permanent political roles.


Anti-Manipulation Safeguards

The system must include protections against coordinated manipulation of reputation.

Possible safeguards include:

- weighting evaluations based on evaluator reputation
- detecting repeated mutual rating patterns between specific users
- reducing the influence of tightly connected rating clusters
- flagging abnormal reputation growth for review

These mechanisms help ensure that reputation reflects genuine contribution rather than coordinated manipulation.

import ollama

def analisar_contributo(nome, tipo, detalhe, valor_estimado):
    prompt = f"""
    És o Auditor de Ética da comunidade Moral Money.
    Analisa o seguinte contributo e explica por que razão este membro deve ter soberania total:
    
    Membro: {nome}
    Tipo de Contributo: {tipo}
    Detalhe: {detalhe}
    Valor Estimado: {valor_estimado}
    
    Regra de Ouro: O benefício real para a sobrevivência coletiva é o que conta. 
    Se um investidor de 60 anos traz infraestrutura (irrigação/estufas), ele tem o mesmo mérito 
    que um jovem que trabalha 5 anos, pois ambos viabilizam a vida da comunidade.
    """
    
    try:
        response = ollama.chat(model='llama3', messages=[
            {'role': 'user', 'content': prompt},
        ])
        return response['message']['content']
    except Exception as e:
        return f"Erro ao ligar ao Ollama: {e}"

# Teste com o teu exemplo:
print("\n--- ANALISANDO CONTRIBUTO ---")
print(analisar_contributo(
    "Investidor Sénior (60 anos)", 
    "Capital/Infraestrutura", 
    "Sistema de irrigação inteligente e estufas industriais", 
    "4.000.000€"
))
